# Homepage Patterns

Use this reference when the homepage role affects the composition more than the
visual style alone.

## Marketing Surface

Use when the homepage is primarily a persuasive front door.

- Lead with product value, not documentation taxonomy.
- Keep the first screen outcome-focused.
- Use proof quickly: screenshots, example output, logos, or product cues.
- Keep top-level calls to action explicit.
- Delay dense caveats and deep reference groupings until below the fold.

## Orientation Surface

Use when the homepage should help users understand where to start.

- Lead with what the project is and who it is for.
- Put the recommended first path above the fold.
- Keep categories mutually clear.
- Use short explanatory blurbs for navigation choices.
- Prefer real repo-derived paths over abstract marketing copy.

## Handbook Entry

Use when the homepage is the opening page to a practical body of documentation.

- Lead with purpose and operating model.
- Show the most important sections early.
- Keep visual tone calm and operational.
- Use hierarchy to support scanning over persuasion.
- Avoid pretending the page is a product launch surface.

## Product-Doc Hybrid

Use when the homepage must both position a product and orient users into docs.

- Split the first screen between product understanding and next-step clarity.
- Show one concrete example or quick-start early.
- Treat the homepage as distinct from downstream article templates.
- Let the hero carry more personality than the reading surfaces.
- Keep the docs entry points obvious even when the page is more brand-forward.

## Pattern Checks

Before finalizing a homepage approach, confirm:

- the first screen matches the page's real job
- users can tell where to go next
- the page does not bury the core quick-start or orientation path
- the composition does not confuse marketing and documentation roles

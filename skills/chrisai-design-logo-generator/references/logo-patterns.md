# Logo Patterns

Use these patterns as concept anchors, not rigid templates.

## Strong Pattern Families

### Singular Geometric Core

One dominant circle, ring, capsule, polygon, or cutout-based form.

### Modular Node System

Two to five nodes with intentional hierarchy and spacing.

### Monogram Reduction

Reduce one or two initials into a single crisp silhouette.

### Negative-Space Cut Symbol

Use subtraction to create the symbol rather than adding many shapes.

### Directional Flow Mark

Use curved or repeated elements that imply movement or transfer.

## Pattern Rules

- prefer one strong focal point
- keep 40 to 50 percent of the canvas visually calm
- use asymmetry intentionally
- keep line weights structurally stable
- avoid decorative clutter

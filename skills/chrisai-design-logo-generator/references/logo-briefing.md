# Logo Briefing

Keep logo intake concise and decision-oriented.

## Required Inputs

- brand or product name
- category or market
- core concept
- positioning or tone

## Helpful Inputs

- color constraints
- visual aversions
- existing symbol ideas
- need for monogram, symbol, or combination mark

## Framing Rule

Translate the brief into brand behavior, not just adjectives.

Examples:

- `trustworthy` may imply stable geometry, open counters, and calm spacing
- `fast` may imply directional tension, forward lean, or flow
- `developer-first` may imply modular logic, precise forms, and restraint

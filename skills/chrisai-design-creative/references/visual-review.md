# Visual Review

Use this checklist before treating the creative direction as stable.

## Hierarchy

- Does the homepage read like a homepage rather than a raw doc page?
- Is the primary message obvious before secondary navigation or ornament?
- Do headings, support text, and calls to action have clear rank?

## System Coherence

- Do background treatments stay coherent across full-width areas?
- Does the spacing system feel deliberate across hero, navigation, and content areas?
- Do decorative elements clarify tone or hierarchy instead of adding noise?

## Reading Surfaces

- Are article and reference pages calmer than the homepage where appropriate?
- Do code blocks remain readable first?
- Do screenshots and diagrams support the page instead of overpowering it?

## Navigation And Semantics

- Do navigation labels describe real destinations accurately?
- Is the page role clear from layout and labeling?
- Does the site avoid visually implying flows or destinations that do not exist?

## Theme And Brand Fidelity

- If both light and dark themes exist, do both feel intentionally designed?
- Does the system still feel like the local product or brand rather than a transplanted reference?
- Has visual personality stayed compatible with legibility and trust?

If the answer to any of these is no, revise the direction before treating the
handoff as complete.

# Brand Refresh

Use this workflow when the main problem is updating or modernizing an existing
brand while preserving useful equity.

This workflow produces a refresh plan and change map. It does not own a full
new brand kit, logo generation, legal/trademark review, or marketing launch
execution unless the user asks for those follow-up deliverables.

## Ownership

This workflow owns:

- current-brand assessment
- preserve/change decisions
- old-to-new positioning, voice, messaging, visual, and asset mapping
- migration risks and rollout recommendations
- routing to narrower branding workflows

This workflow does not own:

- source-backed market research; use `workflows/brand-research.md`
- full kit creation; use `workflows/brand-kit-generation.md`
- logo creation; use `workflows/logo-generation.md`
- raw asset-file QA; use `workflows/brand-asset-audit.md`
- launch planning beyond brand readiness

## Workflow

Work through these steps in order:

1. Check current brand materials.
2. Clarify refresh intent.
3. Identify what to preserve.
4. Identify what to change.
5. Map old-to-new brand decisions.
6. Produce the refresh plan.
7. Route implementation follow-ups.

## Step 1: Check Current Materials

Look for existing brand kits, strategy docs, messaging, website/app copy,
logos, visual assets, screenshots, decks, social profiles, launch notes, and
customer or stakeholder feedback. Do not assume a particular folder exists.

Use `workflows/brand-kit-audit.md` first when the existing brand guide itself
needs a structured completeness or consistency review.

Use `workflows/brand-asset-audit.md` first when the main uncertainty is asset
quality, master files, transparency, favicon readiness, or export gaps.

## Step 2: Clarify Refresh Intent

Capture or infer:

- reason for refresh: outdated look, changed audience, changed product,
  repositioning, rename, acquisition, category shift, quality upgrade, or
  inconsistency cleanup
- refresh intensity: light polish, messaging refresh, visual refresh, naming
  refresh, full rebrand, or staged migration
- constraints: must-keep name, logo, colors, typography, tagline, domain,
  customer recognition, or stakeholder requirements
- target surfaces: website, product UI, docs, decks, social, marketplace,
  app icon, packaging, or internal material

Ask with concise options. Do not assume the user wants a full rebrand.

## Step 3: Preserve Equity

Identify what should likely stay:

- recognizable name or verbal anchor
- trusted logo element or silhouette
- valuable color or type association
- audience trust signals
- strong proof points
- useful language customers already know
- distinctive category position
- assets with implementation value

Separate evidence-backed equity from stakeholder preference.

## Step 4: Identify Change Areas

Identify what should change:

- unclear or stale positioning
- generic category language
- weak or inconsistent voice
- unsupported message claims
- dated visual cues
- poor favicon or small-size logo performance
- inaccessible color or type choices
- scattered or contradictory source files
- missing usage rules or implementation handoff

Flag changes that need research, legal review, design review, or customer
validation.

## Step 5: Map Old To New

Create a change map:

| Area | Current State | Keep | Change | Reason | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Positioning | [current] | [keep] | [change] | [why] | [risk] |

Cover only relevant areas:

- name and domain
- positioning
- audience
- voice
- messaging
- logo
- color and typography
- imagery/iconography/layout
- assets and exports
- brand kit/source of truth

## Step 6: Produce Refresh Plan

Output:

- refresh objective
- preserve/change summary
- old-to-new map
- recommended workflow sequence
- risks and dependencies
- quick wins
- decisions needed from the user

Keep the plan implementation-oriented. A refresh plan should make clear what
changes first and what should wait.

## Step 7: Handoff

End with the next useful step:

- `workflows/brand-research.md` for market or competitor uncertainty
- `workflows/brand-strategy.md` for positioning refresh
- `workflows/brand-voice.md` or `workflows/brand-messaging.md` for verbal
  identity refresh
- `workflows/logo-generation.md` for mark revision or favicon-safe variants
- `workflows/brand-visual-guidelines.md` for visual rules
- `workflows/brand-kit-generation.md` for a revised brand kit
- `workflows/brand-launch-readiness.md` before public rollout

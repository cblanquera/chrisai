# Brand Strategy

Use this workflow when the main problem is defining or refining the strategic
foundation for a brand before voice, messaging, visual guidelines, or a full
brand kit are created.

This workflow produces strategy guidance, not competitor research, logo
concepts, final visual identity, website copy, ads, or legal clearance.

## Ownership

This workflow owns:

- brand purpose, promise, and positioning
- category and audience framing
- differentiation and proof-point synthesis
- values, personality, and strategic principles
- audience/profile implications for later kit sections

This workflow does not own:

- competitor and market research; use `workflows/brand-research.md`
- voice and tone systems; use `workflows/brand-voice.md`
- message libraries; use `workflows/brand-messaging.md`
- visual usage rules; use `workflows/brand-visual-guidelines.md`
- full kit assembly; use `workflows/brand-kit-generation.md`
- logo generation, trademark review, or name availability decisions

## Workflow

Work through these steps in order:

1. Check existing context.
2. Complete only the missing intake.
3. Decide whether research is needed first.
4. Draft the strategy foundation.
5. Stress-test the strategy.
6. State handoff recommendations.

## Step 1: Check Existing Context

Before asking questions, look for existing product, marketing, research, brand,
planning, website, pitch, or strategy material in the workspace. Do not assume
a particular folder exists.

Use existing context first. Ask only for missing inputs that would materially
change the strategy.

## Step 2: Intake

Capture or infer:

- subject: brand, company, product, service, campaign, or idea
- stage: idea, MVP, launched product, rebrand, rename, category expansion, or
  campaign/sub-brand
- audience: founders, developers, SMBs, enterprise buyers, consumers,
  creators, agencies, local customers, or another group
- category: what shelf the brand belongs on and how customers search for it
- goal: positioning, brand kit, website rewrite, launch, investor narrative,
  sales clarity, or internal alignment
- constraints: existing name, domain, logo, colors, voice, legal constraints,
  markets, or stakeholder preferences

When asking, offer concise options instead of open-ended forms.

## Step 3: Decide If Research Comes First

Use `workflows/brand-research.md` first when:

- competitors, category conventions, or market language are unknown
- differentiation depends on claims that need evidence
- the user wants source-backed recommendations
- current public positioning or competitor movement matters

Continue without research only when the user provides enough context or asks
for a provisional strategy draft. Label assumptions clearly.

## Step 4: Draft The Strategy Foundation

Produce the narrowest useful set:

- brand purpose: why the brand exists beyond selling
- brand promise: what outcome the audience can expect
- positioning: audience, category, primary value, and differentiated reason
- audience/profile summary: who matters most and what they care about
- problem and tension: what makes the brand necessary
- differentiation: how this brand should be meaningfully distinct
- proof points: facts, capabilities, customer evidence, or credible signals
- personality: 3-5 traits with behavioral meaning
- values or principles: decision rules, not generic virtues
- strategic implications: what this means for voice, messaging, and visuals

Prefer specific, defensible statements over polished abstractions.

## Step 5: Stress-Test The Strategy

Before finalizing, check:

- Is the category clear enough for a customer to understand?
- Is the audience specific enough to guide decisions?
- Is the differentiation meaningful, believable, and ownable?
- Are proof points tied to claims?
- Are assumptions labeled?
- Could this strategy guide voice, messaging, and visual choices?

If the strategy relies on unsupported claims, mark them as assumptions or route
back to brand research.

## Step 6: Handoff

End with the next useful step:

- use `workflows/brand-voice.md` for tone, personality expression, and writing
  rules
- use `workflows/brand-messaging.md` for one-liners, pillars, taglines, CTAs,
  and boilerplate
- use `workflows/brand-visual-guidelines.md` for visual-system guidance
- use `workflows/brand-kit-generation.md` to assemble the strategy into a
  bundled kit

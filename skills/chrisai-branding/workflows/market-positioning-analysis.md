# Market Positioning Analysis

Use this workflow when the main problem is synthesizing competitor profiles or
brand research notes into market position, category patterns, white space,
messaging gaps, and differentiation opportunities.

This workflow produces synthesis, not competitor discovery, raw competitor
profiles, comparison-page copy, sales battle cards, or final brand kits.

## Ownership

This workflow owns:

- category and positioning pattern analysis
- repeated and overused claim detection
- positioning map or narrative creation
- white-space and market-gap synthesis
- differentiation opportunities and risks

This workflow does not own:

- finding competitors; use `workflows/competitor-discovery.md`
- profiling individual competitors; use `workflows/competitor-profiling.md`
- final brief assembly; use `workflows/brand-research.md`

## Workflow

Work through these steps in order:

1. Confirm input quality.
2. Compare competitors across dimensions.
3. Identify patterns and gaps.
4. Choose positioning axes or narrative.
5. Produce synthesis and recommendations.

## Step 1: Confirm Input Quality

Use competitor profiles, source notes, user-provided research, or prior brand
research. Before synthesizing, confirm:

- competitors are classified when relevant
- material claims have sources or inference labels
- current public claims were verified live when current accuracy matters
- evidence gaps and caveats are visible

If profiles are missing or inconsistent, use
`workflows/competitor-profiling.md` first.

## Step 2: Compare Across Dimensions

Compare competitors across:

- category labels
- target customers
- value propositions
- positioning angles
- message themes
- product promises and capabilities
- proof and trust signals
- pricing and packaging patterns
- voice and tone patterns
- visual identity patterns
- customer language and perception signals
- strengths, weaknesses, and notable gaps

Use the same dimensions across the set so the synthesis is defensible.

## Step 3: Identify Patterns And Gaps

Identify:

- repeated category language
- overused claims
- common value propositions
- credibility signals everyone relies on
- pricing or packaging patterns
- tone and voice patterns
- visual identity patterns
- underserved customer segments
- underused proof points
- white-space positioning opportunities
- risks, unknowns, and assumptions

Do not treat a gap as an opportunity unless it connects to the subject brand,
audience, or research goal.

## Step 4: Choose Positioning Axes Or Narrative

Use positioning axes that fit the category, such as:

- simple vs comprehensive
- technical vs approachable
- premium vs value
- enterprise vs self-serve
- niche vs broad platform
- speed vs control
- automation vs human service

Do not force a 2-axis map when the evidence does not support one. Use a
narrative market map when that is clearer.

## Step 5: Produce Synthesis

Produce the useful pieces for the requested scope:

- category pattern summary
- competitor matrix synthesis
- positioning map or narrative
- repeated language and overused claims
- market gaps and white-space opportunities
- differentiation recommendations
- risks, unknowns, and confidence levels
- implications for brand strategy, voice, messaging, and visual guidelines

End by returning to `workflows/brand-research.md` for brief assembly or to
`workflows/brand-strategy.md` when the user wants to turn the synthesis into a
brand strategy.
